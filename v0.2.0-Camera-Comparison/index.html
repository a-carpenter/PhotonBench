PhotonBench

A Camera Simulation Tool v0.2.0

SNR is an important quantity that allows for a compact comparison of a camera's hypothetical performance under different illumination conditions. Here SNR is plotted as a function of incident photons and as a function of 13 µm pixels (Normalized SNR). Pixel size is important here as, generally, larger pixels will be more sensitive due to the larger surface area. Thus, the Normalized SNR plot is created by scaling the SNR by the ratio of the pixel surface area to a reference 13 µm pixel. The latter plot provides a more accurate comparison of two cameras with different pixel sizes under the same illumination conditions.
